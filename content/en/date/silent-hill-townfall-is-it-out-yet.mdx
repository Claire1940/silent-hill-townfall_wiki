<Section icon={Swords} title="Setting and Story Details">
Silent Hill: Townfall takes players away from the familiar American town to a brand new, equally unsettling locale: a fictional Scottish island named **St Amelia**. The game is set in the 1980s, with the story unfolding in a small town perpetually shrouded in a thick grey fog and sea mist. This environment is perfectly crafted for the psychological torments and monstrous encounters the series is known for.

The developers, Screen Burn, based St Amelia on the real village of St Monans in the East Neuk of Fife, meticulously recreating its features. The real-life "Haar" (sea fog) that affects the Scottish coast plays a significant role in the game's atmosphere.

The narrative is a standalone story, separate from the mainline Silent Hill games, but it retains the core elements: creepy fog, psychological horrors, and manifestations of the protagonist's psyche. Players will control **Simon Ordell**, who mysteriously awakens on St Amelia with an IV bag and medical wristband. His goal is to "put things right," guided by voices picked up on a CRTV he possesses.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Gameplay and Development Insights">
Silent Hill: Townfall is a psychological horror game played from a first-person perspective, putting players directly into Simon's foggy nightmare. The gameplay blends classic Silent Hill mechanics with new twists. Players will engage in combat using both firearms and melee weapons, solve environmental puzzles, and strive for survival against grotesque monsters.

A unique gameplay feature involves Simon's CRTV. Beyond its narrative role in delivering voices, the CRTV also provides tactical information by showing the outlines of enemies in its static, even when they are behind cover. This adds an interesting layer to stealth and combat encounters.

The game is being developed by **Screen Burn**, formerly known as No Code, a Scottish studio with a strong reputation for narrative-driven games like *Stories Untold* and *Observation*. Their involvement, coupled with publishing by Annapurna Interactive and Konami, suggests a strong focus on storytelling and atmospheric horror. Screen Burn has been working on the game since 2020.
