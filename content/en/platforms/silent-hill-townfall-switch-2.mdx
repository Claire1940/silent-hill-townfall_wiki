<Section icon={Swords} title="Gameplay Mechanics: The CRTV and Survival">

Silent Hill: Townfall shifts the series to a first-person perspective, a move that enhances the intimacy of its psychological horror. Unlike previous entries that relied on a simple radio to detect enemies, Townfall introduces the **CRTV (Portable Television)**. This device serves as your primary survival tool, allowing Simon Ordell to tune into unstable analog signals that reveal hidden threats and story fragments.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Signal Tuning">
    Use the CRTV to scan for frequencies. Clear signals can reveal the location of St. Amelia's inhabitants or the "Otherworld" entities stalking you.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Environmental Observation">
    The first-person view allows for tactile puzzle solving. You must physically interact with objects, looking for clues hidden in the 1996-era technology.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Evasion and Stealth">
    Combat is situational. Use the "peek" mechanic to check around corners. Often, the CRTV will tell you a threat is near before you can see it in the fog.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Resource Management">
    Manage your tools carefully. Whether it's a melee weapon or a rare firearm, every encounter in St. Amelia carries the weight of consequence.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The gameplay loop emphasizes "observation first, action second." Players must navigate the cold, isolated island of St. Amelia by reading the environment. The CRTV acts as an evolution of the classic radio, providing visual static and audio cues that represent the protagonist's mental state and the town's shifting reality.

| Mechanic | Classic Silent Hill | Silent Hill: Townfall |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Perspective** | Fixed / Third-Person | First-Person |
| **Threat Detection** | Pocket Radio (Audio) | CRTV (Audio/Visual Static) |
| **Setting** | US (Maine/Generic) | Scotland (Island) |
| **Combat** | Direct / Clunky | Tactical / Evasive |

<Callout type="success" title="Survival Tip">
Always raise your CRTV before entering a new building or a dense patch of fog. The signal distortion is often the only warning you get before a chase begins.
</Callout>
