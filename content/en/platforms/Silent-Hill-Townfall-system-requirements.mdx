<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Step-by-Step Setup Guide for PC">

Preparing your PC for Silent Hill Townfall involves more than just meeting the hardware list. Because the game utilizes a unique CRTV mechanic—a portable television that tunes into unstable analog signals—visual clarity and driver stability are paramount. Follow these steps to ensure your system is ready for the September launch.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Update Graphics Drivers">
    Download the latest Game Ready drivers from NVIDIA or Adrenalin software from AMD. Manufacturers often release specific patches for Silent Hill titles to optimize fog rendering.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Verify Storage Integrity">
    Ensure you have at least 85 GB of free space (75 GB for the game plus 10 GB for temporary shader cache). Use an NVMe drive for the fastest loading times.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Configure Windows 11 Settings">
    Enable "Game Mode" in Windows settings and ensure "Hardware-Accelerated GPU Scheduling" is turned on to maximize the efficiency of your RTX or RX card.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Calibrate HDR and Brightness">
    Townfall uses deep blacks and grey fog. Proper calibration of your monitor's brightness is essential to see the subtle clues transmitted through the CRTV pocket television.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Setup Tip">
If you are playing on a laptop, ensure you are connected to a power source and set the power plan to "Best Performance" to prevent the CPU from throttling during heavy fog scenes.
</Callout>

</Section>
