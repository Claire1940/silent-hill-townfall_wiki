<Section icon={Tv} title="Core Mechanics: The CRTV and First-Person Survival">

Survival in St. Amelia requires more than just a weapon; it requires the ability to interpret unstable signals. The **CRTV pocket television** acts as the spiritual successor to the classic Silent Hill radio, but with expanded functionality for the first-person viewpoint.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Signal Tuning">
    Use the CRTV to scan for analog frequencies. When the static clears, you may uncover hidden story fragments or the location of a nearby threat.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Environmental Peeking">
    The first-person camera allows for a "peek" mechanic. Use this to check around corners in the abandoned clinics and foggy docks of St. Amelia without exposing yourself.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Evasion and Stealth">
    Not every creature can be defeated. The CRTV will emit specific visual distortions when an entity is hunting you, signaling that it is time to hide or find a distraction.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Tactile Puzzles">
    Puzzles are narrative-driven and often require interacting with 1990s technology. Expect to manipulate dials, wires, and CRT monitors to progress.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Survival Tip">
Always keep your CRTV raised when entering a new room. The visual static is often a more reliable indicator of danger than the actual sound, especially in the wind-swept outdoor areas of the island.
</Callout>

</Section>
