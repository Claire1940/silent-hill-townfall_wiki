<Section icon={Swords} title="Gameplay Mechanics: CRTV, Combat, and Stealth">
**Silent Hill Townfall** introduces a blend of classic survival horror elements with new mechanics. The CRTV (Cathode Ray Tube Television) is a central device, reimagining the classic Silent Hill radio for detecting threats and uncovering narrative clues. Gameplay emphasizes first-person exploration, with players needing to master stealth, evasion, and strategic combat to survive the horrors of St. Amelia.

### Core Gameplay Loop

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Explore in First Person">
    Navigate the eerie environments of St. Amelia directly through Simon Ordell's eyes. This perspective enhances immersion and makes clue hunting and close-range encounters more impactful.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Master the CRTV">
    Utilize the portable CRTV to tune into unstable analog signals. This allows you to detect nearby threats, pick up vital story fragments, and find mysterious clues scattered throughout the town.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Assess Threats and Plan Ahead">
    Before engaging, use the CRTV and the peek mechanic to evaluate enemy positions. Many situations reward patience and stealth over direct confrontation.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Strategic Combat">
    Engage in combat using both melee weapons for close quarters and firearms for ranged encounters. Combat is designed to be tense and situational, not a default solution to every enemy.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Evade or Fight for Survival">
    When overwhelmed, prioritize evasion. Break line of sight, use the environment for cover, hide, or distract enemies. Fighting should be a last resort or when a clear advantage presents itself.
  </Step>
  <Step num="6" title="Solve Narrative Puzzles">
    Puzzles are integral to the story, revealing more about Simon, St. Amelia, and the underlying truths. Look for environmental cues and CRTV signals to progress.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="CRTV vs. Classic Radio">
The CRTV functions as an evolution of the traditional Silent Hill radio, providing not just auditory cues but also visual distortions and fragmented images, making threat detection and clue gathering a more dynamic experience.
</Callout>
