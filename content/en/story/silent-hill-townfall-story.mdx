<Section icon={Droplet} title="Simon Odell: A Protagonist Haunted by Guilt">
Simon Odell, born October 15, 1959, is 37 years old at the time the game is set. His journey to Saint Amelia is not voluntary but a summons to confront a dark past. He is seen with a hospital wristband and an IV drip, suggesting a recent accident or medical condition. This medical imagery, combined with his narration, "I keep trying to fix what happened, but no matter what I do," strongly points to a core theme of unresolved guilt. The woman's reply, "You can't fix it. It doesn't let us change things," further solidifies this motif.

The game's setting, Saint Amelia, is not merely a backdrop but an extension of Simon's psyche. The town's memorial plaque, dedicated "for those we lost," and protest banners like "Whatever heart this town had has stopped" suggest a collective tragedy that mirrors Simon's personal torment. His American accent in a Scottish setting also raises questions about his past connection to the town, possibly hinting at a professional role that placed him in a position of responsibility during the incident.
