<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Master the CRTV: Silent Hill Townfall Secrets of the Signal">

The CRTV pocket television is the most important addition to the franchise's toolkit. Much like the radio in previous Silent Hill games, the CRTV alerts you to nearby danger, but it offers far more depth through analog signal tuning. Uncovering **Silent Hill Townfall secrets** requires a mastery of frequency manipulation to reveal hidden messages and track enemies through the thick Scottish fog.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Signal Detection">
    When the CRTV emits static or a high-pitched whine, raise the device immediately. This indicates a nearby threat or a narrative "echo" trapped in the environment.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Manual Frequency Tuning">
    Use the analog sticks or touch controls to tune the dial. Matching the waveform on the screen clarifies the audio and visual feedback, revealing the specific nature of the signal.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Triangulation">
    Rotate Simon's body while the CRTV is active. The signal strength peaks when you are facing the source of the anomaly, allowing you to pinpoint items or enemies.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Decoding Fragments">
    Some signals contain visual data. Clear the static to watch short, distorted clips of past events in St. Amelia, which provide essential clues for progression.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The CRTV uses a color-coded and waveform-based system to communicate information. Learning these patterns is vital for surviving the more intense encounters in the later chapters of the game.

| Signal Waveform | Meaning | Player Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Jagged Red Static** | Hostile Creature | Hide or prepare for combat |
| **Soft Blue Pulse** | Memory Fragment | Stay still and listen to the audio |
| **Steady Green Line** | Safe Zone / Save Point | Approach to secure progress |
| **Erratic White Noise** | Environmental Puzzle | Look for interactable objects nearby |

<Callout type="tip" title="Tactical Advantage">
The CRTV can be used while peeking around corners. This allows you to check for enemy waveforms without fully exposing Simon to the creature's line of sight.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Survival Tactics: Combat, Stealth, and Evasion">

Combat in Silent Hill Townfall is designed to be a last resort. Simon is not a soldier; he is a vulnerable man in a terrifying situation. The game emphasizes stealth and environmental awareness over direct confrontation. When an encounter is unavoidable, you must use your limited resources with absolute precision.

| Survival Method | Best Use Case | Risk Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Stealth (Crouching)** | Navigating narrow corridors | **Low** - Harder to detect |
| **Environmental Distraction** | Moving enemies away from doors | **Medium** - May attract others |
| **Melee (Pipe/Knife)** | Finishing off isolated, weak foes | **High** - Requires close proximity |
| **Firearms (Pistol)** | Emergency space creation | **Very High** - Loud and limited ammo |

The "Peek" mechanic is a central pillar of survival. By holding a shoulder button, Simon can lean around obstacles to observe enemy patrol patterns. Combined with the CRTV's signal detection, this allows for a methodical approach to exploration that minimizes risk.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Observe Patrols">
    Wait in shadows and watch how the creature moves. Most enemies in St. Amelia follow predictable paths unless alerted.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Distract">
    Throw objects like bottles or bricks to pull an enemy's attention away from your objective path.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Execute or Evade">
    If the path is clear, move quickly. If spotted, break the line of sight immediately and find a hiding spot like a locker or under a bed.
  </Step>
</Steps>
