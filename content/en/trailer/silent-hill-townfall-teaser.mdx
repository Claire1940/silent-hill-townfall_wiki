<Section icon={Target} title="The CRTV Mechanic: A New Survival Tool">

One of the most innovative features highlighted since the **silent hill townfall teaser** is the CRTV (Cathode Ray Tube Video) pocket television. This device acts as an evolution of the classic *Silent Hill* radio, providing both utility and narrative depth.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Signal Detection">
    As you move through St. Amelia, the CRTV will pick up static. Increasing signal strength indicates a nearby threat or a hidden story fragment.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Manual Tuning">
    Players must manually adjust the frequency using the analog dials (or PS5 DualSense motion). Finding the "sweet spot" reveals clues or detects enemy positions.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Visual Feedback">
    Unlike the radio, which only provided audio, the CRTV screen shows distorted silhouettes of enemies, allowing you to "see" through walls or dense fog.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Narrative Decoding">
    Certain puzzles require you to tune into specific frequencies to watch brief, VHS-style clips that explain the history of the location you are currently exploring.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Tactical Advantage">
Using the CRTV while in the "Peek" mechanic allows Simon to track enemy patrol routes without exposing himself, which is essential for survival in high-difficulty areas.
</Callout>

The CRTV is not just a tool; it is a manifestation of the game's themes. The "Townfall" refers to both the literal fall of the town and the falling signals that Simon must capture to survive. Managing the device's battery and signal clarity is a core part of the survival horror loop.

</Section>
