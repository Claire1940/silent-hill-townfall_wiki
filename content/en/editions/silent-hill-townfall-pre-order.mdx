<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Core Gameplay Mechanics: The CRTV System">

Unlike the third-person cameras of the *Silent Hill 2 Remake*, Townfall is a strictly first-person experience. This perspective is designed to make the environment feel claustrophobic and to highlight the tactile nature of the game's puzzles. The CRTV (Cathode Ray Tube Valve) pocket television is the centerpiece of the gameplay loop.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Signal Detection">
    As you explore St. Amelia, the CRTV will emit static. You must manually tune the dials to find the correct frequency, which reveals hidden environmental clues.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Enemy Tracking">
    The CRTV acts as an evolution of the classic radio. It detects the presence of "Otherworld" creatures, with the screen static becoming more violent as threats approach.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Stealth and Evasion">
    Simon is not a soldier. Use the peek mechanic to look around corners. If an enemy spots you, your best option is often to break line of sight and hide in the environment.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Narrative Puzzles">
    Puzzles are solved by overlaying CRTV signals onto physical objects in the world. This "signal-shifting" reveals hidden text or paths not visible to the naked eye.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Combat Caution">
While melee weapons and firearms are available, ammunition is extremely scarce. Combat is designed to be a last resort that leaves Simon vulnerable and exhausted.
</Callout>

</Section>
